The Call of Duty franchise, launched in 2003 by Infinity Ward and published by Activision, is one of the best-selling video game series ever,
with over 400 million copies sold. Known for its annual releases, the series includes popular sub-series like Modern Warfare, Black Ops, and Warzone,
which reached 100 million players in just over a year. Initially set during World War II, Call of Duty has since explored modern and futuristic warfare.
The games feature voice acting by celebrities such as Kevin Spacey and Kit Harington, and boast a significant esports presence through the Call of Duty League.
Renowned for its cinematic single-player campaigns and innovative multiplayer modes, the franchise has had a profound cultural impact, influencing other media and popular culture.

The Call of Duty franchise, known for its record-breaking launches and extensive sales, has evolved significantly since its 2003 debut.
It introduced the popular Zombies mode in 2008 and has since included diverse settings from World War II to futuristic scenarios. Despite facing controversies,
like the "No Russian" mission in Modern Warfare 2, it remains a cultural phenomenon. The series features advanced AI, physics, and extensive multiplayer customization,
including cross-platform play. Call of Duty: Mobile expanded its reach, while cinematic trailers and regular updates keep its massive global community engaged.
Its mechanics have influenced numerous other games, solidifying its impact on the gaming industry.
